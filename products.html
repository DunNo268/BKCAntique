PICTURE OF MONA LISA

Mona Lisa painting by Leonardo da Vinci. This is one of the most famous portraits in the world, painted in oil on a poplar panel in Florence in the 16th century, during the Italian Renaissance.

WINGED VICTORY OF SAMOTHRACE

Winged Victory of Samothrace is an ancient Greek marble sculpture depicting the god Nike, the god of victory. Carved in 190 BC, and is considered one of the most famous sculptures in the world.

OIL PAINTING “THE BATTLE OF THE MEDUSA BOAT”

Raft of the Medusa is an oil painting by the French Romantic painter Théodore Géricault (1791-1824) between 1818-1819. The painting was completed when he was only 27 years old and became an icon of French romanticism.

"AMORE-E-PSICHE" PICTURE

The statue of Amore-e-Psiche, made by sculptor Antonio Canova in 1793, depicts two gods in Roman mythology: Cupid and Psyche.

"THE SEATED SCRIBE" PICTURE

The Seated Scribe is one of the most important works of ancient Egyptian art. The statue depicts a letter sitting at work. Carved between 2620-2500 BC, this statue was discovered at Saqqara during the 1850s.

OIL PAINTING “DEATH OF SARDANAPALUS”

Death of Sardanapalus is an oil painting on canvas completed by artist Eugène Delacroix in 1827. The painting was inspired by the king Sardanapulus, who ordered all his wealth to be destroyed after his army was defeated.

"LIBERTY LEADING THE PEOPLE" PICTURE

Liberty Leading the People is an 1830 work by Romanticist painter Eugène Delacroix, inspired by the July Revolution of 1830 in Paris.

COPY OF THE PICTURE “SLEEPING HERMAPHRODITOS”

Replica of the "Sleeping Hermaphroditos" statue dating from the 2nd century, reconstructed by sculptor Gianlorenzo Bernini in 1619. This is the exact size replica of the ancient Greek bronze statue.

"THE MONEYLENDER AND HIS WIFE" PICTURE

The painting "The Moneylender and His Wife" was painted by the artist Quentin Metsys in 1514. The Dutch artist detailed the scene of a greedy couple charging money for items to lend at interest.

VENUS DE MILO

Venus de Milo is the most famous ancient Greek sculpture depicting Aphrodite, the Greek goddess of love and beauty. The statue is sculpted on marble material, slightly larger than the real person with a height of 203 cm, but has lost the original two arms and pedestal.

"LE SACRE DE NAPOLEON" PICTURE

Le Sacre de Napoléon is a painting by Jacques-Louis David, the official artist of Napoleon Bonaparte, completed in 1807. The work is very large, nearly 10 meters long and more than 6 meters high, depicting the ceremony to ascend. Emperor of Napoleon Bonaparte, held at Notre Dame Cathedral in Paris on December 2, 1804.

REBELLIOUS SLAVE

Rebelious Slave is a 2.15m high marble statue carved by the sculptor Michelangelo between 1513 and 1516. It is one of several unfinished slaves at the tomb of Pope Julius II.

OIL PAINTING “LA GRANDE ODALISQUE”

“La Grande Odalisque” is an oil painting completed in 1814 by artist Jean Auguste Dominique Ingres and depicts an Odalisque, or concubine. At 88.9 cm × 162.56 cm, this work was met with much criticism in its initial release.

"AN OLD MAN AND HIS GRANDSON" PICTURE

The painting "An Old Man and his Grandson" completed by Domenico Ghirlandaio in 1490, during the Renaissance period, is considered one of the best works of the Italian painter. rated as the most unique of the 15th century portraits.
